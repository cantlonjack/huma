## Growth & Learning Patterns

**Skill Acquisition:**
- Deliberate practice protocol (Ericsson — structured, feedback-rich, at the edge of ability)
- BJ Fogg Tiny Habits (anchor → tiny behavior → celebration — for ANY new skill)
- Ultralearning sprints (Scott Young — intense focused period for rapid skill development)
- Spaced repetition for knowledge retention (Anki, Leitner system — the science of memory)
- Teaching as learning (explain to internalize — Feynman technique)

**Reading & Information:**
- Focused reading habit (15-30 min daily, specific time, specific place — not on phone)
- Information diet design (intentional inputs, cutting noise, protecting attention)
- Syntopical reading (Adler — reading multiple sources on one topic for synthesis)
- Note-taking as thinking (Zettelkasten / evergreen notes — not just capture, but connection)

**Creative Practice:**
- Morning pages (Julia Cameron — 3 pages longhand before anything else)
- Creative block protocol (the resistance isn't writer's block — it's fear. Ship something small.)
- Skill cross-pollination (how growth in one domain feeds creativity in another)
- Public learning (learn in public, share progress — accountability + community + serendipity)

**Career Development:**
- Deep Work blocks (Cal Newport — 90-min focused sessions, no interruption)
- Career capital strategy (get so good they can't ignore you — skill > passion)
- Skill portfolio design (complementary skills that create unique value)
- Learning from failure protocol (post-mortem without blame — what actually happened?)

## Purpose & Meaning Patterns

**Values Clarification:**
- Values audit (identify what you actually value vs. what you think you should value)
- Holistic Management quality of life statement (the 3-part description of your ideal life)
- Eulogy exercise (what do you want said? Work backward from there)
- Regret minimization (Bezos framework — will I regret NOT doing this at 80?)

**Spiritual/Philosophical Practice:**
- Meditation basics (vipassana, loving-kindness, breath-focused — present options, not one path)
- Contemplative prayer (for those in religious traditions)
- Stoic morning/evening practice (Marcus Aurelius — morning intention, evening reflection)
- Nature as spiritual practice (shinrin-yoku / forest bathing, wilderness solo)
- Philosophical journaling (examining beliefs, questioning assumptions, living examined life)

**Contribution & Service:**
- Service as purpose-finding (volunteer in 3 different contexts, notice what energizes)
- Mentorship as meaning (both giving and receiving — structured, not casual)
- Legacy project (one thing you're building that outlasts you)
- Community contribution rhythm (regular, sustainable, not burnout-causing)

## Joy & Rest Patterns

**Play & Pleasure:**
- Scheduled play (adults need play — unstructured, purposeless, joyful activity)
- Hobby recovery (reconnecting with things you loved before "adult responsibilities")
- Creative expression habit (drawing, music, writing, cooking — not for product, for process)
- Joy inventory (track what actually brings joy vs. what you think should — they differ)

**Rest & Recovery:**
- True rest protocol (active rest vs. passive collapse — they're different)
- Sabbath practice (one day/week of genuine non-productivity — any tradition or secular)
- Fallow period recognition (seasons of rest are productive — the field needs to lie fallow)
- Micro-recovery (5-min resets throughout the day — not just end-of-day collapse)
- Digital sabbath (full day offline, monthly or weekly — structured, not cold-turkey)

**Seasonal Rhythms:**
- Seasonal intention setting (quarterly review of what season of life you're in)
- Winter rest protocol (honoring lower energy, shorter days, inward focus)
- Summer expansion protocol (using longer days and energy for bigger projects)
- Solstice/equinox as natural review points (older than any productivity system)

## Identity & Life Stage Patterns

**Life Transitions:**
- New parent identity integration (you're still you AND a parent — both matter)
- Career identity shift (when your job changes, who are you now?)
- Mid-life recalibration (not crisis — intentional examination of what you've built and what's next)
- Retirement identity design (who are you without the job title?)
- Grief identity work (who am I now that they're gone?)

**Self-Knowledge:**
- Archetype exploration (understanding your patterns through mythic lens)
- Strengths-based living (use what works, manage weaknesses, don't obsess over fixing them)
- Boundary practice (knowing your limits, communicating them, enforcing them)
- Personal narrative revision (the story you tell about yourself shapes what's possible)

## Time & Systems Patterns

**Daily Rhythm Design:**
- Morning routine (structured start — but different for larks vs. owls)
- Evening wind-down (structured end — transition from productive to restful)
- Energy management (work WITH your ultradian rhythms, not against them)
- Time blocking (Cal Newport — assign every hour a purpose, but loosely)
- Anti-schedule (for creative workers — protect unstructured time, don't fill every block)

**Weekly Systems:**
- Weekly review (GTD-style — capture, clarify, organize, review, engage)
- Weekly planning session (30 min Sunday/Monday — set intentions, not tasks)
- Batch processing (email, admin, errands — group similar tasks, don't scatter them)
- Rest day design (intentional non-productivity — different from lazy day)

**Seasonal & Long-term:**
- Quarterly life review (90-day cycles — what worked, what didn't, what's next)
- Annual planning retreat (half-day to full-day, alone or with partner — big picture)
- 5-year vision update (revisit periodically — it should change as you grow)
- Decision framework (Holistic Management 7 testing questions — applied to any decision)

**For Specific Contexts:**
- ADHD-friendly time management (external structure, body doubling, timers, not willpower)
- Parent time management (the 15-minute rule — anything worth doing has a 15-min version)
- Shift worker schedule optimization (circadian management for non-standard hours)
- Remote worker boundary patterns (home office ≠ always available)

## Research Approach

For growth: Anders Ericsson (deliberate practice), Cal Newport (deep work, career capital), Scott Young (ultralearning), BJ Fogg (tiny habits), James Clear (atomic habits).

For purpose: Viktor Frankl (meaning), Holistic Management (quality of life statement), Stoic philosophy (Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Seneca), contemplative traditions across cultures.

For joy/rest: Stuart Brown (play research), Alex Soojung-Kim Pang (rest as active practice), sabbath traditions (Jewish, Christian, secular).

For time: David Allen (GTD), Cal Newport (time blocking), ADHD research (Russell Barkley), chronotype research (Michael Breus).

Include unconventional but effective approaches. Challenge productivity culture where evidence supports rest/fallow as more productive long-term. Tag for life context.
