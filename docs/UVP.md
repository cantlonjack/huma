# HUMA · Unique Value Proposition

*Locked April 2026. Change only with intention — every future page, email, pitch, and product decision is calibrated against what's below.*

---

## Primary UVP (full form)

For investor decks, about pages, pitches, the first paragraph of the founder's bio.

> **HUMA is the life strategist for anyone running their own life. It sees your whole situation — money, time, body, people, work — and shows you the one move this week that cascades through the most of what matters, drawn from practice that worked for people in situations like yours.**

## Landing hero (short form)

For the landing page, first line above the fold, the tweet.

> **The life strategist that sees your whole situation and shows you where the leverage is this week.**

## One-line bio

For founder Twitter/X, LinkedIn headline, email signature, Substack byline.

> **HUMA — the leverage finder for anyone running their own life.**

## Tagline

Everywhere small. Footers. Business cards. The bottom of the manifesto.

> **See the whole. Find the leverage. Practice what works.**

---

## Audience — the wedge

People running their own life as one system. Freelancers, solopreneurs, creators, independent professionals, self-employed parents, small-business owners, people between jobs, people leaving institutions. No employer or institution buffers them from the consequences of bad decisions across money, time, body, people, work. They already know their life is one system; HUMA tells them where the leverage is in it.

The architecture generalizes to anyone. The wedge is self-employed because the pain is sharpest there and the willingness to pay is already present.

## Against alternatives

| Alternative | What it does | What HUMA does differently |
|---|---|---|
| Therapy | Personal, human, $200/hr | Scales; validated across populations; persists between sessions |
| Self-help books | Static frameworks, generic | Contextual; compiled to your exact situation |
| Life coaches | Variable, not compounding, $$$ | Draws on every validated practice; gets smarter for you as the commons grows |
| AI chatbots (Claude, ChatGPT) | Brilliant but no memory, no methodology | Persistent whole-life context; structured ontology; validated patterns |
| Habit trackers | Track what you do | Tells you the one thing that cascades, not just tracks |

## What HUMA is NOT

To keep the UVP honest:

- Not a therapist, doctor, or financial advisor
- Not a life coach
- Not a chatbot
- Not a habit tracker
- Not a productivity tool
- Not a wellness or meditation app

If any feature drifts HUMA toward one of these, it fails the UVP and should be redesigned or cut.

## Test for whether the UVP works

1. **Read the primary form to a friend.** Ask them to explain back what HUMA does. If they can say it in their own words, the language is clear. If they say *"wait, so is it a therapist? a habit tracker? an AI?"* the language is still too abstract.
2. **Ask: "Does this sound like something you'd want to try?"** A yes means the promise lands. A "what is it exactly?" means the words aren't carrying the thing yet.
3. **Show it alongside three competitors** (a habit tracker, a therapy app, Claude). Ask which they'd pick. HUMA should be the one that doesn't feel like any of the others.

If any test fails, redraft. Do not ship a landing page with a UVP that hasn't passed all three.

## Rationale — how each word was chosen

- **"Life strategist"** — from the fiduciary framing ("works for you, in your corner"). Chosen over *partner* (too warm), *coach* (HUMA is explicitly not a coach), *assistant* (too subservient), *advisor* (too clinical).
- **"For anyone running their own life"** — the wedge. Narrower than *for humans*; broader than *for freelancers*. Captures the structural position (no institutional buffer) rather than a demographic.
- **"Sees your whole situation"** — the keystone differentiator. Every fragment-app sees one slice; none sees the whole. "Whole" echoes the tagline on purpose.
- **"Money, time, body, people, work"** — five of HUMA's nine dimensions, chosen for universal recognition. Omitted on purpose: home, growth, joy, purpose, identity — not because they're absent, but because listing five is concrete, nine is a taxonomy.
- **"The one move this week"** — the leverage/node framing, operationalized as a weekly cadence the operator can act on.
- **"Cascades through the most of what matters"** — the connected-system framing, expressed in the operator's terms (what matters to them) rather than HUMA's terms (capital dimensions).
- **"Drawn from practice that worked for people in situations like yours"** — the commons and validation mechanism, delivered as a promise rather than a lecture about the architecture.
