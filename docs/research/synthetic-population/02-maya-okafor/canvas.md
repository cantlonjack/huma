## Your Essence

Maya is a maker who builds clarity from complexity. She spent nine years designing interfaces for other people's products — first at a mid-size agency where she learned to read client problems three screens deeper than the brief, then in-house at a fintech startup where she could see how a premature decision in onboarding cascaded into abandonment at checkout. What she carries from that work is the ability to see how systems connect, a precise eye for what's essential and what's noise, and a quiet confidence that the right structure makes hard things feel simple.

What brings her alive is making patterns visible. Before the career took over, she painted — acrylics on canvas, mostly abstract, the kind of work where she was translating the logic she saw everywhere into color and form. She stopped when Kofi was born. Not a dramatic decision — the mornings filled up and never emptied again. She signed up for an online painting course last year and made it through two lessons. She showed up to a printmaking session at Spudnik Press and didn't go back. The itch is still there every time she opens a blank page. The painting and the systems thinking are not separate skills — they are the same capacity expressed in different media.

What she needs right now is not inspiration. She needs structure. The layoff cracked open everything at once: income, identity, daily rhythm, physical health. She has three and a half months of runway, $42K in student debt, a six-year-old son who needs her steady and present, and a body that stopped moving when the stress arrived. She is not starting from zero. She has a decade of professional skill, a rent-stabilized apartment in one of Chicago's best neighborhoods, a school community that shows up with groceries without being asked, and the rare honesty to say "I'm rebuilding everything at once" without pretending it's fine.

What can sustain her is a designed life — not a job, but a practice where freelance UX work pays the bills, creative work feeds the soul, physical movement restores the body, and the financial pressure has a visible, shrinking trajectory. She does not need to find herself. She needs to build the container that lets everything she already is show up in the same week.
