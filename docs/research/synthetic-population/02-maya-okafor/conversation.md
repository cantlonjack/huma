## Phase 6: Operational Design

**HUMA:** Here's your week. It's built around two fixed points: Kofi's school schedule and your morning sequence.

**Every weekday, 6:00-6:30 AM:** Movement. Non-negotiable. This is the foundation the rest stands on.

**6:30-7:00 AM:** Drawing practice. Twenty minutes, sketchbook at the kitchen table, while Kofi eats breakfast. Not production — practice. Some mornings it's bad. Do it anyway.

**7:00-8:30 AM:** Breakfast with Kofi, walk to school, settle into your desk.

**8:30 AM-3:00 PM, Monday through Thursday:** Client work. Deep design blocks Monday and Wednesday. Client calls and lighter work Tuesday and Thursday. Thursday afternoon shifts to illustration or creative projects after noon — that's the one block where the creative practice gets a full session, not just morning minutes.

**Friday:** Buffer day. Client overflow, prospecting, personal projects, errands. Kofi has a 2:30 pickup on Fridays, so this is a shorter day.

**3:00/3:30 PM:** Hard stop. Walk to school. The rest of the day is Kofi's.

**Saturday:** Family day. Longer morning movement. Kofi time. Afternoon for illustration if you want it, or rest.

**Sunday:** Slow morning. Free afternoon. 7:00 PM: the money ritual, thirty minutes. 7:30 PM: preview the week. 8:00 PM: done. The evening is yours.

The protected blocks are: the morning sequence (move + draw), the Thursday afternoon creative session, and the Sunday money ritual. Everything else can flex. These can't.

**Maya:** I notice there's no evening work in this. That feels unrealistic — what about when a client needs something?

**HUMA:** Then you do it. But you design the system so that's the exception, not the norm. If you're regularly working evenings, the rate is wrong or the scope is wrong. Both are fixable. The evening is the last thing you protect, and the first thing that tells you the system needs redesigning.

Your validation checks, weekly: How many mornings included movement? Target: five or more. How many mornings included drawing? Target: five or more. Did you pick up Kofi by 3:30 every day? Target: five of five. Did the Sunday money ritual happen? Target: every week. When one of these slips, don't add guilt. Ask what changed. That's a design question, not a character question.

Seasonally: spring is pipeline building and establishing the morning routine — the money pressure is highest, the practice is newest, protect the morning sequence even when it feels like you should be sending proposals instead. Summer means Kofi's out of school — plan the childcare in May, camps and co-ops with school parents, or the schedule falls apart in July. Fall means school is back, routine is established, the temptation is to fill every hour with client work because the energy returns. Resist. Thursday afternoons are how the illustration practice becomes real. Winter is for rest, reflection, holiday illustration prints if you want them, and not letting January feel like starting over. The morning routine carries through the holidays. Five minutes counts. Continuity beats intensity.

You didn't lose your ability to make sense of complexity. You just stopped applying it to your own life.
