# H U M A
## User Journey Map
### Every Touchpoint from First Visit to Graduation

*This document maps the complete operator experience. Every screen, every emotion, every data flow, every transition. Features built in isolation feel like fragments. Features built along this journey feel like one coherent experience.*

*Updated March 2026 — Progressive Depth Model (aligned with HUMA_PRODUCT_SURFACE.md)*

---

## 01 — The Journey at a Glance

```
DISCOVER → ARRIVE → SHAPE BUILDER → DAILY PULSE → PATTERN RECOGNITION
    ↓                                                      ↓
 (days)                                        ONE-THING CARD (day 5+)
                                                           ↓
                                                     MAP CONVERSATION
                                                           ↓
                                                  THREE TABS (full depth)
                                                           ↓
                                                  SEASONAL EVOLUTION
                                                           ↓
                                                      GRADUATION
```

The journey has nine stages. Each stage has a critical transition — a moment where the operator either continues or churns. Design effort should be concentrated at these transitions.

**The three critical transitions (where most people are won or lost):**
- Shape Builder → Daily Pulse (do they come back day 2?)
- Daily Pulse → Map Conversation (do they accept the invitation at week 2?)
- Map Conversation → Three Tabs (do they engage with the one-thing card?)

---

## 02 — Stage 1: Discover

**How they find HUMA:** A shared shape (capital radar comparison) on social media. An essay about regenerative life design. A friend's recommendation. A search for "whole life design tool" or "regenerative enterprise planning."

**What they see first:** The shared shape artifact or the landing page. In both cases, the visual identity (sage/sand/amber, organic radar shapes, spatial canvas) is the first impression. They should know within 3 seconds that this is NOT a wellness app, a productivity tool, or a generic SaaS product.

**Emotional target:** Curiosity + recognition. "This describes something I've been feeling but couldn't name."

**Critical transition → Arrive:**
The operator clicks through to the landing page. The landing page must convert curiosity into a single action: try it.

**What can go wrong:**
- The landing page explains too much (they bounce before understanding)
- The visual identity is generic (they categorize it as "another app" and leave)
- The shared shape isn't visually distinctive enough to stop the scroll

---

## 03 — Stage 2: Arrive (Landing Page)

**Duration:** 15-60 seconds.

**What they experience:** The landing page story — hero → problem → demo → how it works → contrast → canvas preview → vision → CTA.

**Emotional arc:** "This is for me" → "Something IS off" → "Oh — it shows you the connections" → "I want to try that" → "Let me start."

**Data flow:** None. No account creation. No email capture. No cookies consent wall. Zero friction between curiosity and action.

**Critical transition → Shape Builder:**
They click "See your shape" and arrive at /begin. This is NOT a 40-minute commitment. It's a 90-second visual assessment — the Shape Builder. The threshold is as low as possible.

**What can go wrong:**
- Two competing CTAs dilute the action
- The page asks for email before demonstrating value
- The "how it works" section implies a long time commitment
- The problem section doesn't name THEIR pain specifically enough

---

## 04 — Stage 3: First Touch (Shape Builder)

**Duration:** 90 seconds.

**What they experience:** The Shape Builder — a visual, 8-card assessment. Each card represents one dimension (Money, Home, Body, People, Growth, Joy, Purpose, Identity). The operator taps a level for each. No typing. No account creation. No explanation of what HUMA is.

On completion, HUMA shows their shape (capital radar) immediately and responds with three things:
- **The Connection:** Names a link between dimensions they thought were separate
- **The One Thing:** A concrete, doable action for today
- **The Invitation:** "Want to keep going? Come back tomorrow — I'll check in."

**Emotional target:** Surprised recognition. "How did it know that?" The coherence recognition moment — happening in 90 seconds, not 40 minutes. The visual shape makes it tangible.

**Data captured:** Their 8-dimension self-assessment. Under the surface, HUMA identifies dimension coupling, shape asymmetries, and candidate patterns. None of this is shown explicitly. The operator sees their shape and feels understood.

**Critical transition → Daily Pulse:**
They leave. They will either come back tomorrow — or they won't. The invitation is warm, not pushy. Within 24 hours, a gentle nudge: "Quick check-in? 30 seconds."

**What can go wrong:**
- The shape response feels generic (doesn't name THEIR specific connections)
- The one-thing is too vague or too ambitious
- The page asks for account creation before delivering value
- The invitation feels like a marketing hook rather than genuine care

---

## 05 — Stage 4: Daily Pulse (Days 2-14)

**Duration:** 30 seconds per day.

**What they experience:** A quick check-in showing 2-3 dimensions most relevant to what they shared on day one. Tap 1-5 for each. Done.

```
Quick check — how's today?

Body    [1——2——3——4——5]
Money   [1——2——3——4——5]
People  [1——2——3——4——5]

[Done]
```

After 3-4 days, HUMA starts seeing patterns: "Your Body dips every Wednesday. What happens on Wednesdays?"

These small insights build trust and demonstrate that HUMA sees things they don't.

**Emotional target:** Building trust through accumulated small moments. "It remembers. And it's right."

**Data flow:** Each pulse creates a data point in the operator's timeline. After 3-4 days, patterns become detectable. After 5-7 days, the one-thing card may appear alongside the pulse.

**Critical transition → Pattern Recognition:**
Do they keep coming back? The 30-second commitment is key — low enough to sustain daily. Each day's insight makes the next day's return more likely.

**What can go wrong:**
- The pulse asks too many dimensions (overwhelming, not 30 seconds)
- HUMA's pattern detection is too slow (no insight by day 4 = no trust)
- The insights are generic rather than connected to their specific data
- No notification/reminder brings them back on day 2-3

---

## 06 — Stage 5: Pattern Recognition (Days 5-10)

**Duration:** Organic — happens within daily pulse interactions.

**What they experience:** HUMA's insights get noticeably more specific. Connections they hadn't seen. "Your sleep improved 2 nights after you did the expense list. Money clarity → better sleep. That's not random."

This is the magic moment — but it happens gradually through accumulated data, not in a marathon conversation. The coherence recognition deepens each day.

**Emotional target:** Growing trust + dawning realization. "This thing actually understands my life as a system."

**Data flow:** Enough daily pulse data to detect multi-dimensional coupling. Pattern matching against the growing context model.

**Critical transition → Map Conversation:**
At approximately day 10-14, HUMA extends the invitation: "I've been watching the patterns in your check-ins. I think I see your whole picture forming. Want to map it together? Takes about 15-20 minutes."

This invitation is EARNED, not pushed. The operator has experienced value for 2 weeks. They're curious what the full picture looks like.

**What can go wrong:**
- The invitation comes too early (before trust is built)
- The invitation comes too late (operator plateaus on daily pulse)
- The invitation feels like an upsell rather than a natural deepening
- Pattern recognition is too shallow to justify the deeper conversation

---

## 07 — Stage 6: Map Conversation (Week 2-3)

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes. (Was 40 minutes in the original design — now shorter because HUMA already has 2 weeks of context.)

**What they experience:** The 6-phase conversation, but warmer and faster because HUMA references what they've already shared. "You've been tracking Money and Body for two weeks. Let's see the whole picture — who are you beyond those two dimensions?"

The conversation still follows the phase structure internally (ikigai → context → situation → patterns → actions → rhythm). The operator experiences it as a natural deepening.

**Emotional target:** Excitement + recognition. "Oh — all of this connects."

**Data flow:** Phase transitions store context syntheses and canvas data. The accumulated daily pulse data enriches every phase. On completion, all data flows into the canvas.

**Critical transition → Canvas Moment:**
The conversation ends. The "See your map" button appears. This click must feel like a reward — 2+ weeks of daily engagement crystallizing into a tangible whole-life artifact.

**What can go wrong:**
- The AI doesn't reference prior pulse data (feels disconnected from daily experience)
- The conversation is too long despite having existing context
- The canvas build isn't visible during the conversation
- Phase transitions feel abrupt rather than natural

---

## 08 — Stage 7: Canvas Moment (The Glasses Moment)

**Duration:** 5-15 minutes of exploration.

**What they experience:** Their spatial canvas — the center-outward map showing Essence → What Matters → What Makes It Work → Practices → Where I'm Going. Warmth system active: elements connected to their 2 weeks of daily data are already emerging; untested elements are faint.

**Emotional target:** Awe + ownership. "This is MY life. I can SEE it." The coherence recognition feeling at maximum intensity. The canvas is partly warm (tested through daily pulses) and partly faint (new from the conversation) — visually honest about what's real and what's aspiration.

**Interactions available:**
- Tap any element to see detail, connections, warmth history
- See the shape (capital radar) as a background overlay
- Share the shape or canvas URL
- Toggle to document view

**Data flow:** Canvas generated and stored with shareable URL. The shape is generated for sharing.

**Critical transition → Daily Practice:**
The operator now has the canvas AND the daily one-thing card. Do they engage with the one-thing card as a daily practice? This is where HUMA becomes a tool they use, not a thing they tried.

**What can go wrong:**
- The canvas is just a formatted document (no spatial layout, no warmth system)
- The warmth doesn't reflect their actual daily engagement (feels disconnected)
- There's no bridge from the canvas moment back to the daily card
- The share artifact isn't visually compelling

---

## 09 — Stage 8: Daily Practice (Ongoing)

**Duration:** 30 seconds daily + 30-60 seconds weekly.

**What they experience:** The one-thing card each day. One action. Hard stop reminder. Done / Not today. No guilt.

Sunday: weekly shape morph (30-60 sec default). The operator sees their shape shift over the week — which dimensions moved, which held. One insight card. Coherence score. "Sounds right" / "That's not it." Optional: "Go deeper" for 5-8 minutes of full validation review.

The canvas warms over time. Elements that hold up become solid. Elements that don't become faint or shift. The operator watches their map become more real.

**Emotional target:** Quiet confidence. The ritual of daily engagement becomes its own reward. The weekly insight creates micro-moments of coherence recognition.

**Data flow:** Daily one-thing responses, weekly insight feedback, optional deep review data. All feeding warmth calculations and pattern detection. Shape evolving over time.

**Key moments in daily practice:**
- **Week 2-3:** First multi-week pattern surfaced
- **Week 4-6:** Connection insight the operator missed
- **Week 8-10:** Systemic adjustment suggestion
- **Week 12:** Seasonal review invitation

**Critical transition → Seasonal Evolution:**
After 12 weeks, the shape has changed visibly. HUMA invites the seasonal review. The visual difference between week 1 and week 12 IS the value proposition.

**What can go wrong:**
- The one-thing card becomes repetitive
- Weekly insights plateau in specificity
- The warmth system doesn't update visibly enough
- No re-engagement bridge when the operator misses days

## 10 — Stage 9: Seasonal Evolution

**Duration:** 20 minutes, quarterly.

**What they experience:** A conversation that reviews the season:
1. The Evolution Question: "What do you know now that you didn't know when this season started?"
2. What held, what shifted, what surprised
3. Canvas update — new commitments, revised practices, evolved understanding
4. Next season's shape produced (feeds the timeline)
5. Next season's one-thing priorities identified

**Emotional target:** Perspective + growth. Not "you did great" — but "look how far the system has come. Look what you've learned."

**Data flow:** Seasonal synthesis stored. Canvas updated. Shape timeline extended. If the operator chooses, their key insight is structured as a pattern contribution to the commons.

**The sharing moment:** Shape comparison — January vs. now. Two radar images. Visually different. The operator texts it to a friend: "Look at this." The friend opens HUMA. Types what's on their mind. The cycle begins again.

**Graduation (measured passively, never displayed):**
After 2-4 seasonal reviews, some operators internalize whole-context thinking. They see connections naturally. They make holistic decisions by instinct. They stop using HUMA — and that's success. They tell five people about it.

## 11 — The Sharing Moments

At each stage, there are natural moments where the operator is most likely to share:

| Stage | What They Share | Why | Where |
|-------|----------------|-----|-------|
| Shape Builder | A screenshot of their shape | "This thing mapped my life in 90 seconds" | Text to friend, social media |
| Canvas Moment | The shape or canvas URL | Pride in seeing the whole picture | Text to partner/friend, social media |
| Week 4-6 | A weekly insight screenshot | "This thing sees connections I missed" | Social media, group chat |
| Seasonal Review | The shape comparison | Visual proof of growth over time | Social media, newsletter, blog |
| Year in Review | The 52-week shape animation | Spotify Wrapped for life | Everywhere |

**The primary share artifact is the shape** — the capital radar at two points in time. Simple. Beautiful. Unique to every person. Every shared shape is distribution.

## 12 — The Churn Points (Where We Lose People)

| Transition | Churn Risk | Defense |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| Landing → Shape Builder | High (70%+ bounce expected) | The problem section must name THEIR pain. 90-second visual entry = zero commitment. |
| Shape Builder → Day 2 Pulse | Very high | The shape + connection insight must land. The invitation to return must feel natural. 24-hour nudge. |
| Daily Pulse → Map Conversation | High | Pattern recognition by day 5 builds trust. The invitation at week 2 is earned, not pushed. |
| Map Conversation → Three Tabs | Medium | The one-thing card bridges canvas insight to daily action. Canvas warmth creates feedback loop. |
| Month 2-3 → Seasonal Review | Medium | Seasonal review creates a "fresh start" moment. Shape comparison creates visual payoff. |
| Free → Paid | Medium | Paid tier (one-thing card + compiled methods + deep review) must deliver obvious daily value beyond the free insight. |
