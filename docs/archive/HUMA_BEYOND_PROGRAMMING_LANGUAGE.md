## The Name Problem

RPPL — Reality Pattern Programming Language — is wrong on two 
counts. "Programming" implies code. "Language" implies words. 
What we're actually describing is neither.

What is it?

Mathematics isn't a language. It's not code. It's a NOTATION 
SYSTEM for perceiving and communicating quantitative structure. 
Before mathematical notation existed, humans could see "more" 
and "less" but couldn't communicate "exactly how much more, 
in what ratio, with what rate of change." Math didn't create 
quantity. It created the ability to PERCEIVE and COMMUNICATE 
quantity precisely.

Music notation isn't a language. It's not code. It's a SYSTEM 
for perceiving and communicating temporal pattern. Before 
notation, music existed — but it couldn't be transmitted 
precisely across time and distance. Notation didn't create 
music. It made music TRANSFERABLE.

What we're building is a notation system for practical wisdom. 
It doesn't create wisdom. It makes wisdom PERCEIVABLE, 
COMMUNICABLE, and TRANSFERABLE.

Some naming directions:

**Drop "Programming":**
- Reality Pattern Language (RPL) — but Alexander already owns 
  "Pattern Language" in people's minds
- Reality Pattern Notation (RPN) — accurate but sounds technical

**Drop the acronym entirely:**
- The Pattern Commons — describes the library, not the notation
- The Wisdom Protocol — describes the system, not the perception

**Name the CAPACITY, not the tool:**
- Whole Sight — what you gain (but sounds mystical)
- Pattern Literacy — what you develop (parallel to "literacy" 
  and "numeracy")

**Pattern Literacy** might be the answer.

Literacy = the ability to read and write language.
Numeracy = the ability to read and write quantity.
**Pattern Literacy** = the ability to read and write the 
practical patterns of living.

You can be pattern-literate without any app. You can be 
pattern-literate in any language. Pattern literacy is the 
CAPACITY. RPPL (or whatever we call the notation) is the 
SYSTEM that teaches it. HUMA is ONE MEDIUM that transmits it.

But the name of the notation itself... let's hold that. 
Maybe it emerges. Maybe it stays RPPL for the technical 
layer and "pattern literacy" for the human-facing concept. 
Maybe the community names it. Maybe it doesn't need a name 
at all — mathematics wasn't called "mathematics" by the people 
who invented it. They just called it "thinking clearly about 
quantity."

## The Multiple Mediums

If the notation is real — if it describes something true about 
how human lives work — then it should be expressible in ANY 
medium. Not just software.

### Medium 1: The App (HUMA)
The digital mirror. Helps you see your own patterns through 
the shape, the conversation, the daily practice. The most 
scalable medium. The tutor.

### Medium 2: The Board Game (Regenaissance)
A cooperative game where players navigate life situations using 
pattern cards, dimensional resources, and collective decision-making. 

Why a board game is powerful:
- **It's physical.** You hold the pattern cards. You move tokens 
  on a board. You FEEL the resource constraints.
- **It's social.** You play with other humans. You see THEIR 
  patterns. You discover that your solution works in their 
  context and theirs works in yours. Cross-pollination happens 
  naturally.
- **It's low-stakes.** You can experiment. "What if I invest 
  all my energy in Body and neglect Money?" Play it out. See 
  what happens. Learn the coupling WITHOUT real consequences.
- **It's offline.** No data. No servers. No surveillance. 
  The pattern language works on a kitchen table.
- **It teaches the grammar viscerally.** After 3 games, you 
  understand coupling, leverage, fields, and nodes — not because 
  someone explained them, but because you PLAYED them.

What Regenaissance could look like:

**Each player has a Life Board** — 8 dimensions, each with a 
resource level (1-5). The shape of your resources IS your shape.

**Pattern Cards** are the main mechanic. Each card is a practice 
you can adopt. It costs resources (time, energy, money) and 
builds resources (across multiple capitals). But the CONTEXT 
matters — a pattern card that works when your Body is 4 might 
fail when your Body is 2. The field conditions are printed 
on the card.

**Event Cards** introduce disruptions. Financial shock. Health 
event. Opportunity. Seasonal change. Just like the simulation.

**The Cooperative Element:** Players share a Community Board. 
When one player validates a pattern (it worked in their 
context), it becomes available to all players at reduced cost. 
THIS IS THE PATTERN COMMONS IN PHYSICAL FORM. The game 
literally teaches the network effect.

**Winning:** Not individual victory. The group wins when the 
average coherence across all players exceeds a threshold. 
You can't win alone. You win by helping each other find 
leverage. Your validated pattern helps them. Their validated 
pattern helps you.

**The real game:** After playing Regenaissance, people start 
seeing their ACTUAL lives this way. "That's a pattern card." 
"My Body is at 2, I need to address that before I can 
take on a new enterprise." "What if we shared this pattern 
with the neighbor?" The game TEACHES the perceptual grammar 
through play.

### Medium 3: The Curriculum
For schools, community groups, workshops. Teaching pattern 
literacy as a life skill. Not a course ABOUT HUMA. A course 
about SEEING.

"Today we're going to draw our shapes." 
"This week we're learning about coupling." 
"For homework: find one node in your own life."

### Medium 4: The Book
The published language. Like Alexander's book. Not a manual 
for the app. A REFERENCE for the pattern language itself. 
The patterns, the grammar, the principles. Anyone can read it, 
learn from it, practice it.

### Medium 5: The Physical Practice
Pattern literacy practiced in community. Like a book club, 
but for life design. A group meets monthly. Each person 
shares: "Here's what I tried. Here's what I learned. 
Here's the pattern I'd contribute." The group validates. 
The pattern enters the commons.

No technology required. Just the language and the practice.

### What all mediums share

- The same perceptual grammar (dimensions, coupling, fields, 
  nodes, patterns)
- The same pattern library (patterns work in any medium)
- The same graduation goal (develop the capacity in the person)
- The same commons model (validated patterns are shared)

The app has advantages (scale, personalization, data). 
The board game has advantages (physical, social, low-stakes). 
The curriculum has advantages (structured learning, mentorship). 
The book has advantages (deep study, permanent reference).

They're all HUMA. They're all RPPL. They're all expressions 
of the same underlying language in different mediums.

## The Pattern Economy

You asked: what if people could earn a living contributing 
validated patterns?

This is the piece that makes it self-sustaining. Not a company 
selling a product. A LIVING ECONOMY where wisdom is the currency.

### How value flows in the current world

1. You figure out something useful through years of practice
2. You write a book about it (maybe)
3. The book sells a few thousand copies
4. The publisher takes 85%. You get 15%.
5. The knowledge reaches a tiny fraction of people who need it
6. Most of it is forgotten within a decade
7. The next person starts from scratch

The value chain is: **Discovery → One-time publication → 
Slow, lossy transmission → Eventual loss.**

### How value flows in the pattern economy

1. You figure out something useful through practice
2. You structure it as a pattern (using the RPPL notation)
3. You contribute it to the commons
4. It gets validated by other practitioners in similar contexts
5. As it's validated, your pattern gains confidence levels
6. Every time someone USES your pattern successfully, you earn
7. Every time someone ADAPTS your pattern, both of you earn
8. The pattern evolves through collective practice — it gets 
   BETTER, not stale
9. The knowledge reaches everyone who needs it, adapted to 
   their context
10. It never gets forgotten because it's living

The value chain is: **Discovery → Structured contribution → 
Community validation → Continuous usage revenue → Living evolution.**

### The economics

**Pattern Royalties:**
When someone uses a pattern through HUMA (or any RPPL-compatible 
tool), a micro-payment flows to the contributor chain:

- Original discoverer: 40%
- Significant adapters: 30% (split among them)
- Validators: 20% (split among people who tested it)
- Commons maintenance: 10%

The payments are small per use. But a pattern used by 
10,000 people generates meaningful income. A pattern used 
by 1,000,000 people generates a living.

**This is the Satoshi insight applied to knowledge.**

Bitcoin miners earn by validating transactions. Pattern 
contributors earn by validating wisdom. The incentive structure 
makes honest contribution more profitable than gaming the system 
(because patterns are validated by PRACTICE, not by votes or 
clicks — you can't fake a practice validation across thousands 
of diverse contexts).

### What this means for contributors

**Richard Perkins** published his enterprise economics in a book. 
He gets royalties on book sales. Maybe $50K over the life of 
the book. The knowledge reaches maybe 50,000 readers.

In the pattern economy: Perkins contributes his enterprise 
patterns to the commons. They're structured in RPPL. They get 
validated by thousands of farmers worldwide. Every time HUMA 
compiles a Perkins-originated pattern for a specific operator, 
Perkins earns a micro-payment. Over time, across millions of 
compilations, Perkins earns more from the pattern economy than 
from all his book sales combined. And the knowledge reaches 
EVERYONE who needs it, adapted to their specific context.

**A grandmother in Oaxaca** discovers that a specific companion 
planting pattern works in her microclimate. She contributes it 
(through a community facilitator or the app). It gets validated 
by 50 practitioners in similar climates. It enters the library 
as a proven pattern. Every time it's used, she earns. Her 
traditional knowledge — which would have died with her — is 
now permanent, attributed, and compensated.

**A freelance designer in Chicago** discovers that batching 
client communication to two daily windows (a time-blocking 
adaptation) transformed her productivity and her stress levels. 
She contributes the pattern. It's validated by 200 freelancers. 
It enters the library. She earns from every compilation. 
Her practical wisdom — which she would have kept to herself — 
now helps every freelancer who faces the same challenge.

### The contributor path

Not everyone contributes patterns. There's a natural distribution:

- **Most people** are PRACTITIONERS — they use patterns, validate 
  them through practice, and provide usage data. They don't 
  contribute new patterns, but their practice validates existing 
  ones and triggers micro-payments to contributors.

- **Some people** are ADAPTERS — they take existing patterns and 
  modify them for new contexts. They earn a share of usage 
  when their adaptation is used.

- **Few people** are DISCOVERERS — they identify genuinely new 
  patterns through their practice and contribute them to the 
  commons. They earn the original contributor share.

- **Very few people** are SYNTHESIZERS — they see connections 
  between patterns across domains and contribute transformers, 
  grammar rules, and meta-patterns. They earn from every 
  compilation that uses their structural insight.

This mirrors how knowledge actually works. Most people use 
knowledge. Some adapt it. Few create it. Very few see the 
deep structure. All of them contribute to the living system.

### Why this can't be gamed

Pattern royalties are based on VALIDATED USE, not on likes, 
clicks, or votes. A pattern earns when:

1. A practitioner applies it in their context
2. The practitioner reports the outcome (positive, negative, 
   or adapted) through whatever medium they're using
3. The outcome is consistent with the pattern's predicted 
   capital impact

You can't fake this at scale. You'd need thousands of people 
to fake positive outcomes across diverse contexts. The 
diversity of contexts IS the defense — a pattern that only 
works in one narrow context is quickly exposed when people 
in other contexts try it and report failures.

This is the same defense that makes Bitcoin hard to attack: 
you'd need to control 51% of the network. In the pattern 
economy, you'd need to fake 51% of the validation data across 
diverse contexts. The more diverse the practitioner base, 
the harder this becomes.

### The currency question

Do pattern royalties flow in dollars? In crypto? In a 
HUMA-specific token?

The simplest answer: dollars, via the subscription revenue.

$29/month for HUMA subscriptions. A portion of that revenue 
flows to the contributors whose patterns that subscriber used 
that month. The more patterns you contributed that are actively 
in use, the more you earn.

A more ambitious answer: a HUMA token that represents pattern 
value. Contributors earn tokens. Tokens can be exchanged for 
fiat or used within the HUMA ecosystem. But this adds complexity 
and regulatory risk. Start with the simple model.

The most Satoshi answer: the pattern library itself is a 
blockchain-like structure where every pattern has an immutable 
provenance chain, every validation is a recorded transaction, 
and the "mining" is the act of validating patterns through 
practice. But this is architecture, not UX. Build the simple 
version first. The infrastructure can evolve.

## The Three-Layer Model (Revised)

### Layer 1: The Notation (RPPL / Pattern Literacy)
The perceptual grammar. Open. Published. Belongs to humanity.
Expressible in any medium: app, game, book, curriculum, practice.

### Layer 2: The Mediums (HUMA app, Regenaissance game, etc.)
Multiple implementations of the notation. Each medium teaches 
pattern literacy through a different modality. The app is the 
most scalable. The game is the most physical. The curriculum 
is the most structured. The book is the most permanent.

HUMA the app is the FIRST medium, not the ONLY medium.

### Layer 3: The Commons (the pattern library + economy)
The living collection of validated patterns. Contributed by 
practice. Validated by use. Attributed to discoverers. 
Compensated through royalties. Available to all. Evolving 
continuously.

The commons is what SURVIVES if every medium disappears. 
The patterns are the knowledge. The mediums are the delivery 
mechanisms. If HUMA the company shuts down, the patterns 
remain. Anyone can build a new medium that reads them.

## What to Build Now

Given all of this — the language, the mediums, the economy, 
the commons — what do we actually build TODAY?

### The honest answer:

**We build the app. But we build it AS IF the language, the 
game, and the economy already exist.**

This means:

1. **The data model is pattern-first, not app-first.** Every 
   pattern in the system is stored in the RPPL notation, not 
   in a HUMA-specific format. Any future medium can read it.

2. **The conversation teaches the grammar, not just the result.** 
   When HUMA shows a coupling between Money and Body, it's not 
   just an insight. It's TEACHING the concept of coupling. When 
   it identifies a node, it's teaching nodal thinking. When it 
   compiles a pattern, it's teaching pattern adaptation. The 
   app is a Socratic tutor for pattern literacy.

3. **The pattern library is open from day one.** Not "we'll 
   open it later." PUBLISH the 12 seed patterns. Publish the 
   schema. Invite contributions. The commons starts now, not 
   after product-market fit.

4. **Attribution is built into every pattern.** From the first 
   seed pattern ("Production Batching — attributed to Richard 
   Perkins, Ridgedale Farm"), every pattern carries its 
   provenance. When the economy launches, the attribution 
   chain is already there.

5. **The graduation metric is real.** Every feature is tested 
   against: does this teach pattern literacy or just deliver 
   a result? The app succeeds when people learn to see.

6. **The shape IS the universal entry point.** It works in the 
   app. It could work on a paper card. It could work as the 
   first round of the board game. The shape is medium-agnostic. 
   It's a snapshot of your field expressed through 8 dimensions.

The board game, the curriculum, the book, the pattern economy — 
those come later. But the app is designed so they CAN come. 
The architecture doesn't assume software. It assumes the 
language.
